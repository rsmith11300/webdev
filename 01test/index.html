Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship?

Digital citizenship refers to the responsible and ethical use of technology and the internet. It encompasses a range of behaviors and practices that individuals should follow to ensure a positive online experience for themselves and others.

What is Digital Law?

Digital law refers to the legal principles and ethical requirements that govern electronic actions and behavior in digital environments.

What is AUP?

An Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) is a set of rules and guidelines established by an organization—such as a school, business, or internet service provider—that defines the proper and improper ways its technology resources can be used.

What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is the act of presenting someone else's words, ideas, or creative work as your own without proper attribution.

Whether you mean to do it or not, it is Plagiarism.

What is Academic Dishonesty?

Academic dishonesty is any action or behavior that undermines the integrity of the educational process by using deceptive means to gain an unfair advantage.

It is a broad term that includes plagiarism as a subset, but also encompasses cheating, fabrication, and other fraudulent acts.

What are the rules for AI Generated Content?

In 2026, the rules for AI-generated content (AIGC) revolve around three pillars: transparency, human accountability, and context-specific use.
